About

Systems and control, learning, and structure in dynamical systems.

I am a Direct-PhD student in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at the University of Toronto. My research focuses on indirect identification of stable linear time invariant systems using adaptive observer based schemes.

I completed my B.E. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the American University of Beirut in 2023 with a 4.0 CGPA. During my studies, I was an E3 research intern at the Adaptive Systems Laboratory at EPFL, working on multi agent reinforcement learning in partially observable environments, and I worked on shared control and human machine interfaces for autonomous vehicles.

I am broadly interested in data driven methods for dynamical systems, and in how mathematical structure can be leveraged to design learning algorithms that come with clear performance and stability guarantees.

Research

Selected areas I am currently thinking about.
  • Adaptive observer based identification. Developing indirect identification schemes for stable LTI systems that pair adaptive observers with classical system identification tools, with an emphasis on convergence proofs and robustness to modeling uncertainty.
  • Learning in decentralized partially observable systems. Studying belief sharing and policy evaluation in decentralized POMDPs, including the effect of communication structure on performance guarantees.
  • Shared control for automated driving. Using HMI design, simulators, and sequence models to study driver intention recognition and safe control handover between human drivers and autonomous vehicles.
  • Data informed teaching and learning. Applying statistical analysis and simple models to inform the design of mixed learning introductory programming courses.
