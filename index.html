about

Direct-PhD student in the Systems Control Group at the University of Toronto, advised by Mireille E. Broucke.

Research area: system identification for stable LTI systems, using adaptive observers and tools from systems & control.

B.E. in Electrical & Computer Engineering from the American University of Beirut (CGPA 4.0, Dean’s Honor List).

Past research experience at the Adaptive Systems Laboratory, EPFL, working on multi-agent reinforcement learning in decentralized POMDPs.

now

Working on indirect identification of stable linear systems via adaptive observers, with a focus on convergence and robustness guarantees.

Ongoing: writing, polishing proofs, and running simulations; TA work in control / robotics courses; occasional time spent thinking about system-theoretic views of learning.

research

Current themes

  • Adaptive observer–based identification for stable LTI systems.
  • Decentralized learning and policy evaluation in partially observable settings.
  • Shared control and human–machine interaction for automated driving.
  • Data-driven analysis of large introductory programming courses.
