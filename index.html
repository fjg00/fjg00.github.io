01
hello

I am a Direct PhD student in the Systems Control Group at the University of Toronto, advised by Mireille E. Broucke.

I work on system identification for stable linear time invariant systems, mostly through adaptive observer constructions and ideas from systems and control.

Before Toronto I studied Electrical and Computer Engineering at the American University of Beirut, graduated with a 4.0 CGPA, and spent a summer at the Adaptive Systems Laboratory at EPFL working on multi agent reinforcement learning in decentralized POMDPs.

03
research
// things my brain keeps looping on
  • Adaptive observer based identification. Indirect schemes for stable LTI systems that combine adaptive observers with classical system identification tools and come with actual convergence and robustness guarantees.
  • Decentralized learning under partial observability. Policy evaluation and belief sharing in decentralized POMDPs and how communication structure affects performance.
  • Shared control and HMI for automated driving. Driver intention recognition and control handover in stop and go scenarios, using simulators and sequence models.
  • Data informed teaching. Using performance and engagement data to understand and improve large mixed format introductory programming courses.
