about

A bit of context

I am a Direct-PhD student in the Systems Control Group at the University of Toronto, working with Mireille E. Broucke.

My current work focuses on system identification for stable linear time invariant systems, mainly through adaptive observer constructions and tools from systems and control.

Before Toronto, I completed a B.E. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the American University of Beirut (CGPA 4.0, Dean’s Honor List), and spent a summer at the Adaptive Systems Laboratory at EPFL as an E3 research intern working on multi-agent reinforcement learning in decentralized POMDPs.

now

What I’m working on

  • Developing an adaptive observer–based indirect identification scheme for stable LTI systems.
  • Refining a submission on this topic for IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control (TAC).
  • Thinking about how observer structure and identification guarantees interact.
  • TA work in control and robotics courses at UofT.
research

Things my work touches

  • Adaptive observer–based identification for stable linear systems, with explicit convergence and robustness guarantees.
  • Decentralized learning under partial observability (policy evaluation, belief sharing, communication constraints).
  • Shared control & HMI for automated driving, especially handover and driver intention recognition in stop–go scenarios.
  • Data-informed teaching in large introductory programming courses.
