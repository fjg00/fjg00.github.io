About Me

I am a Direct-PhD student in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto, working under the supervision of Prof. Mireille E. Broucke.

My fascination lies in the intersection of control theory and learning. I am driven by the challenge of creating mathematical frameworks that allow systems to "know" themselves and their environments accurately. Currently, I am focused on System Identification for Linear Time-Invariant (LTI) Systems, working to build adaptive observers that are both robust and theoretically guaranteed to converge.

Previously, I graduated with high distinction (4.0 GPA) from the American University of Beirut (AUB). I've also had the privilege of researching decentralized multi-agent reinforcement learning at EPFL in Switzerland.

Research Interests

My work generally falls into the bucket of making autonomous systems smarter and safer. Specific areas I am actively working on include:

  • ● System Identification: Indirect identification of stable linear systems using adaptive observers.
  • ● Multi-Agent Systems: Policy evaluation in decentralized POMDPs with belief sharing.
  • ● Human-Machine Interaction: Shared control frameworks for autonomous vehicles (HMI).
