About

A quick snapshot, not a full biography.

I'm a Direct-PhD student in the Systems Control Group at the University of Toronto. My work lives at the intersection of systems & control, learning, and identification, with a bias towards methods that come with clear guarantees.

Before Toronto, I completed my B.E. in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the American University of Beirut, where I graduated with a 4.0 CGPA and spent time thinking about shared control for autonomous driving, reinforcement learning in decentralized POMDPs, and how to make introductory programming courses less painful for students.

Research themes

What my brain is usually occupied with.
  • Indirect identification via adaptive observers. Using adaptive observer structures to indirectly identify stable LTI systems, and proving convergence and robustness properties of the resulting algorithms.
  • Learning in decentralized partially observable systems. Policy evaluation and information sharing in decentralized POMDPs, including belief sharing architectures and communication constraints.
  • Shared control & HMI for automated driving. Driver intention recognition and control handover design for stop-and-go scenarios, using simulators and sequence models.
  • Data-informed teaching. Analyzing performance and engagement data for large scale, mixed-format programming courses to improve how we teach computation.
