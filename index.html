About

Context without being your full CV.

I am a Direct-PhD student in the Systems Control Group at the University of Toronto, advised by Mireille E. Broucke. My current work focuses on indirect identification of stable linear time-invariant systems via adaptive observer constructions, with an emphasis on stability guarantees and robustness.

Before Toronto, I completed a B.E. in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the American University of Beirut (AUB), graduating with a CGPA of 4.0 and Dean‚Äôs Honor List recognition. I spent a summer as an E3 research intern at the Adaptive Systems Laboratory (EPFL) working on multi-agent reinforcement learning in decentralized, partially observable settings, and I have also worked on shared control and human‚Äìmachine interfaces for autonomous driving.

More broadly, I like questions where structure in a dynamical system can be turned into a provably good learning or identification algorithm.

Research themes

What my brain is usually busy with.
  • Adaptive observer‚Äìbased identification. Indirect identification schemes for stable LTI systems that pair adaptive observers with classical system identification tools, aiming for rigorous convergence and robustness guarantees.
  • Decentralized learning under partial observability. Policy evaluation and belief-sharing in decentralized POMDPs, including how communication structures affect performance.
  • Shared control & HMI for automated driving. Driver intention recognition and control handover design for stop-and-go scenarios, using simulators and sequence models.
  • Data-informed teaching & learning. Using performance and engagement data to improve large, mixed-format introductory programming courses.
