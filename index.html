About

System identification, adaptive observers, and learning in dynamical systems.

I am a Direct-PhD student in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at the University of Toronto, advised by Prof. Mireille E. Broucke. My research focuses on system identification for stable linear time invariant systems and the design of adaptive observers with rigorous stability guarantees.

Before joining U of T, I received my B.E. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the American University of Beirut (AUB) with a 4.0 CGPA and Dean's Honor List recognition. I have research experience in multi agent reinforcement learning under partial observability through an internship at the Adaptive Systems Laboratory at EPFL, and I have worked on shared control and human machine interfaces for autonomous driving.

Broadly, I am interested in how structure in dynamical systems can be exploited to design reliable identification and learning algorithms that are both theoretically sound and practically implementable.

Research

A short overview of my current projects and interests.
  • Adaptive observer based system identification. Developing indirect identification methods for stable LTI systems that combine adaptive observer techniques with classical system identification tools, with an emphasis on provable convergence and robustness.
  • Learning in partially observable multi agent systems. Studying policy evaluation and information sharing in decentralized POMDP settings, including belief sharing architectures and distributed evaluation algorithms.
  • Human machine interaction in automated driving. Investigated shared control strategies and driver intention recognition for takeover scenarios, using HMI design, simulators, and deep sequence models.
  • Data driven evaluation of teaching and learning. Analyzed performance and engagement data for large scale introductory programming courses delivered in mixed learning formats.
