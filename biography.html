about me

My name is Fatima Ghadieh (pronounced FAH-tih-ma GHA-dee-eh, where the 'Gh' sounds like a French 'R'). I'm a third-year Direct Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto. I'm supervised by Professor Mireille Broucke. My current research focuses on the continuous time identification of stable linear systems using an adaptive observer. Specifically, my work utilizes concepts from adaptive control, optimization, and advanced linear algebra to develop a framework for model identification. I grew up in Beirut, Lebanon, where I received my B.E. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the American University of Beirut (AUB). Before joining UofT, I was an E3 research intern at EPFL's Adaptive Systems Laboratory, where I collaborated with Professor Ali H. Sayed and Dr. Mert Kayaalp to research multi-agent reinforcement learning.
