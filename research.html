Decentralized Learning in POMDPs

At the Adaptive Systems Laboratory (EPFL), I worked on multi-agent reinforcement learning in decentralized partially observable Markov decision processes (Dec-POMDPs). This included developing methods for policy evaluation under partial observability, studying belief sharing mechanisms between agents, and analyzing how communication structure affects learning in decentralized settings.

Shared Control & Human-Machine Interaction

I studied shared control frameworks for automated driving, focusing on control handover between human drivers and automated systems, and driver intention recognition in stop-go traffic scenarios.

Data-Informed Teaching

I investigated how students interact with mixed-format offerings in large introductory programming courses, using performance data and statistical analysis to inform course design decisions.
