research

Current themes

  • Adaptive observer–based identification. Indirect schemes for stable LTI systems that combine adaptive observers with classical system identification, with an emphasis on convergence and robustness guarantees.
  • Learning under partial observability. Policy evaluation and belief sharing in decentralized POMDPs, and how communication structure changes what is achievable.
  • Shared control and HMI. Driver intention recognition and control handover in automated driving, particularly in stop–go scenarios.
  • Data-informed teaching. Using course data from large introductory programming classes to understand performance and engagement.
background

Where this comes from

I am part of the Systems Control Group at the University of Toronto, working with Mireille E. Broucke. My current work builds on ideas from classical systems and control, observer design, and system identification.

Earlier, I worked at the Adaptive Systems Laboratory (EPFL) on multi-agent reinforcement learning in decentralized POMDPs, and completed my B.E. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the American University of Beirut.
