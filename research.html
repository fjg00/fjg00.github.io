research

Current themes

  • Adaptive observer–based identification for stable linear systems, with explicit convergence and robustness guarantees.
  • Decentralized learning under partial observability, including policy evaluation, belief sharing, and the effect of communication structure.
  • Shared control & HMI for automated driving, especially handover and driver intention recognition in stop–go scenarios.
  • Data-informed teaching in large introductory programming courses, using performance data to understand how students interact with mixed-format offerings.
background

Where this comes from

I am part of the Systems Control Group at the University of Toronto, working with Mireille E. Broucke. My current work combines ideas from classical systems and control, observer design, and system identification.

Before that, I worked at the Adaptive Systems Laboratory (EPFL) on multi-agent reinforcement learning in decentralized POMDPs, and completed my B.E. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the American University of Beirut.
